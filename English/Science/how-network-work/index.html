History of Telephone network

  • Western Union’s network

    • features:
    • lots of stations needed
    • message expressed via Morse code
    • From 1860 - 1861
    • Point-to-Point

  • Bell Telephone

    • features
    • late 1890s
    • Point-to-Point directly to another telephone
    • used switches later to expand
    • hybrid of analog and digital technologies

  • Analog signal to Digital signal

    • Telephone -> Analog signal -> Twisted -pair wire -> Neighborhood Accumulator (Subscriber Line Concentrator, SLC) -> Digital signal -> telephone company switching office (Central Office, CO) -> Another CO -> Digital signal -> Another SLC -> Analog signal -> Another telephone

  • PBX: Private branch exchange

    • like call 1234(a company), then dial 10(say front-desk), this is what a PBX does

  • What’s the problem of punch cards?

    • the result may not be consistent due to the machine fail over time

  • Eearly Terminals, CRT Screen

    • replace mechanical parts with Integrated Circuit(IC)
    • to solve the problem of emergent work out of office, we later have Telephone Network

  • Data Encoding

    • Baudot - 5 bits, 32 different characters
    • ASCII - 7 bits, 128 different characters
    • EBCDIC - 8 bits, 256 different characters
